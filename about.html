Introduction

Abyssinia, known today as Ethiopia, is one of Africa’s most biologically diverse countries. Its varied landscapes include mountains, forests, savannas, and valleys, which support many unique wildlife species found nowhere else in the world.

Unique Animals

Ethiopia is home to several endemic animals such as the Ethiopian wolf, Gelada baboon, and the Walia ibex. These species are specially adapted to the highland environment and are important symbols of the country’s natural heritage.

Birdlife

Abyssinia has over 800 bird species, including endemic birds like the Blue-winged goose and Prince Ruspoli’s turaco. Lakes and rivers also support many water birds and fish.

Water and Aquatic Life

National parks such as Simien Mountains, Bale Mountains, and Awash National Park help protect wildlife and promote eco-tourism. Conservation is important to preserve biodiversity for future generations.
